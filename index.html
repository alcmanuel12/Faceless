Matilde Cherner

Matilde Cherner was a Spanish writer and journalist. She was a woman of strong political convictions and a staunch federal republican. Her most renowned work is María Magdalena (1880).

Louisa May Alcott

Louisa Alcott wrote short, sensationalist novels aimed at adults in the second half of the 19th century (being a true pioneer in the field), such as Behind the Mask (1866) and The Ghost of Abbot (1867).
