“Had I not adopted a man’s name…”
— Mary Ann Evans
Uncovering the women writers of the 19th century who had to hide behind male pseudonyms to be heard. Their names were erased — their words were not.

Fernán Caballero
Cecilia Francisca Josefa Böhl

Renowned for her traditional style and for introducing realism into 19th-century Spanish narratives, with works such as "La Gaviota" (1849) and "La familia de Alvareda" (1856).

Currer, Ellis, y Acton Bell
The Brontë sisters

Considered one of the greatest exponents of 19th-century Romanticism, famous for influential novels such as Jane Eyre (1847), Wuthering Heights (1847), and Agnes Grey (1847).

Rafael Luna
Matilde Cherner

Matilde Cherner was a Spanish writer and journalist. She was a woman of strong political convictions and a staunch federal republican. Her most renowned work is María Magdalena (1880).

A. M. Bernard
Louisa May Alcott

Louisa Alcott wrote short, sensationalist novels aimed at adults in the second half of the 19th century (being a true pioneer in the field), such as Behind the Mask (1866) and The Ghost of Abbot (1867).
